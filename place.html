A Journey Across Divisions

From the ancient monasteries of the north to the Mughal forts of the center and the mosques of the south, explore the architectural marvels that define Bangladesh.

Dhaka

Lalbagh Fort

A 17th-century Mughal fort complex that stands as a testament to the architectural brilliance of the era. It features the tomb of Pari Bibi, a mosque, and the Diwan-i-Aam.

Learn More
Chattogram

Shalban Vihara

An ancient Buddhist vihara in Mainamati, Comilla. Dating back to the 8th century, it was a major center for Buddhist learning and features a central shrine surrounded by 115 monastic cells.

Learn More
Rajshahi

Somapura Mahavihara

A UNESCO World Heritage Site in Paharpur. Highlights the greatness of the Pala Empire, this 8th-century monastery is one of the most important archaeological sites south of the Himalayas.

Learn More
Khulna

Sixty Dome Mosque

Another UNESCO World Heritage Site, located in the historic Mosque City of Bagerhat. It is the largest mosque in Bangladesh from the Sultanate period (15th century).

Learn More
Barishal

Bibichini Shahi Mosque

A historic Mughal-era mosque in Betagi, Barguna. Built in 1659, it sits atop a 30-foot hillock and is named after Bibi Chini, offering a glimpse into the region's spiritual past.

Learn More
Sylhet

Keane Bridge

A historic landmark of Sylhet city spanning the Surma River. Built during the British colonial era in 1936, this steel bridge is an iconic symbol of the region's connection to the past.

Learn More
Rangpur

Tajhat Palace

A historic palace in Rangpur, built in the early 20th century. It is one of the finest preserved examples of feudal architecture in Bangladesh, now housing a museum.

Learn More
Mymensingh

Shashi Lodge

Also known as the Mymensingh Rajbari. Built by Maharaja Suryakant Acharya Chowdhury, this majestic palace is famous for its classical architecture and musical staircase.
